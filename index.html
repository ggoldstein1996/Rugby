What is Rugby?

Rugby, a late-19th century contact sport, is one of the most rewarding and physically demanding activities out there. Like American football, Rugby involves two teams moving an oval-shaped ball to the "end-zone" of the opposing team, marked by an H-shape field goal post. Unlike football, however, a rugby ball can only be moved forward by running the ball or punting it. All passing between teammates must be either lateral or backwards. The effect is a sport centered on speed, brute power, and most importantly, teamwork. While rugby is known as a traditionally British and European sport, it is quickly spreading across the United States.
